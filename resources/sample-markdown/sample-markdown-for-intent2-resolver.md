# Critic Picks: The 10 Must-See 2025 Movies (Updated)

By [Mark Hughes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/markhughes/)
*Contributor.*  
Forbes contributors publish independent expert analyses and insights.  
Mark Hughes is an entertainment writer covering cinema and genre TV.

---

With another year in our rearview mirror and the Oscars just around the corner, it’s time to look back at what cinema had to offer and pick my top 10 and top 20 must-see and best 2025 movies.

2025 wound up offering plenty of great films and exhilarating entertainment to choose from. *[Sinners](https://www.forbes.com/sites/markhughes/2025/04/26/sinners-wins-weekend-box-office-in-stunning-10-drop-and-45-million/)* was an early fantastic offering that stunned audiences and critics alike, while doing blockbuster business. While a few other social-commentary releases like *[One Battle After Another](https://www.forbes.com/sites/markhughes/2025/09/27/review-one-battle-after-another-enters-the-oscar-race-as-mixed-bag/)* earned rave reviews and modest box office returns, films with equally or stronger messages like *[The Running Man](https://www.forbes.com/sites/markhughes/2025/11/13/review-the-running-man-is-a-thrilling-and-faithful-stephen-king-movie/)* wound up unfortunately overlooked and forgotten.

My selections are a mix of purely “Best Picture” contenders, ambitious and standout storytelling and messaging, and outright popcorn escapist delights. It’s a testament to the fact that however hard times get and however much we might say a particular year is “light” on top-shelf cinematic offerings, the truth is usually that there’s just a lot of releases folks don’t hear about or get time to see, but that it’s not hard to find one or two dozen great films from which you could find strong awards contenders and engrossing entertaining viewing.

I’m sure some selections and omissions will prove controversial, but remember I am using my own combination of factors and considerations and pure personal preference (gasp!) in these selections, so there’s bound to be disagreements. But I suggest taking a chance on many of these even if you see some you disagree with or don’t see something you feel should be included, because you’re bound to strike gold.

## The Must-See 2025 Movies

Now without further ado and in no particular order, except that the first 10 are indeed my current top 10 for best films of the year and are roughly how I’d rank them on a ballot at the moment, here are my selections for the top 10 and top 20 must-see and best movies of 2025…

1. **Sinners**  
2. **It Was Just An Accident**  
3. **Train Dreams**  
4. **The Running Man**  
5. **Eddington**  
6. **One Battle After Another**  
7. **Companion**  
8. **Highest 2 Lowest**  
9. **The Perfect Neighbor**  
10. **Bring Her Back**  

Those are the 10 films I’d suggest first, if you haven’t seen them and are looking for the best of the year’s viewing. For my next 10 picks to fill a full top-20 best must-see movies, in no particular order, read on…

11. **Wake Up, Dead Man**  
12. **Superman**  
13. **F1**  
14. **Bugonia**  
15. **Fantastic Four: First Steps**  
16. **Roofman**  
17. **A House of Dynamite**  
18. **Wicked: For Good**  
19. **Weapons**  
20. **Zootopia 2**  

And there you have it, all of my picks for the 20 best movies and must-see films of 2025. Honorable mentions go to **Thunderbolts**, **Sorry Baby**, and **Frankenstein**.

As of this writing, I did not have a chance to see the following films, but very much suspect I will love and retroactively add some of them to my top-20 or top-10 rankings…

1. **The Secret Agent**  
2. **Marty Supreme**  
3. **Hamnet**  
4. **Caught Stealing**  
5. **Avatar: Fire and Ash**  
6. **Sirât**  
7. **My Father’s Shadow**  
8. **If I Had Legs I’d Kick You**  

There’s two or three of those I feel sure will wind up in my top 10 picks, and a couple more likely to fall into my top 20 choices. So I would expect to at least be able to expand to a strong top-25 list if I see two of these additional films soon and added in my honorable mentions.

(UPDATE: The Taiwanese film **Left-Handed Girl** is a revelation and would be in my top three along with **Sinners** and **It Was Just An Accident**, which are all so close to one another they’re practically tied for first place.)

However much it might feel like the year was light on awards-worthy fare, take a moment to really look over all of the films listed here and ask yourself if it’s really hard to pick out at least one if not two Oscar ballots filled with top-tier films.

Now consider just picking *five* films, the way Best Picture ballots worked until 2009’s expansion of the nominees. There are easily five to ten films listed in this article worthy of hypothetical inclusion on a five-film ballot, so I think it’s hard to claim 2025 was a bad year for cinema if it included enough movies to have filled two year’s worth of Oscar ballots in the idealized decades of the 20th Century.

Go back to 1939 or 1979 or any other year from those two decades so (rightly) beloved by cinephiles and historians, and find me two or three Oscar ballots’ worth of movies that would stand the test of time as Best Picture nominees.

I’m just saying, I get the feeling that on the one hand corporate conglomerates headed by tech-bros and people entirely unaligned with storytelling and cinema have bought up Hollywood and focus ever more on child- and family-focused branded IP “content” to sell merchandise, but on the other hand, rich businesses headed by people driven by their own egos and desire for profits always ran studios and brands, sequels, remakes, adaptations, and the same stars in the same roles year after year was always a go-to model driving filmmaking, it’s just more obvious now as cinema has grown so large and frankly expanded into a blurred overlap with top-shelf streaming and POVD/exclusive-streaming films.

Yes, it’s new, and yes, it’s changing things a lot. But it’s progress and it’s more stories from more people for a larger audience, and I have to believe that’s always proven ultimately good for everyone -- and for storytelling itself in the long run. I think this is born out in my picks for the best must-see 2025 movies.
